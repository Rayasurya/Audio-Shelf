The Lighthouse at Quiet Harbor

Chapter One
The keeper climbed the spiral stairs each evening before dusk, carrying a small brass lamp and a pocketful of matches. From the top of the tower she could see the whole harbor, the fishing boats returning in a slow line, and the gulls wheeling above the breakwater. She trimmed the wick, polished the great lens, and waited for the sun to touch the water.

Chapter Two
Winter came early that year, and with it a fog so thick that the boats rang their bells all through the night. The keeper kept the light burning and counted the bells from her chair by the window. One bell was missing. She wrote the boat's name in her logbook, wrapped herself in an oilskin coat, and went down to wake the harbor master.

Chapter Three
By morning the fog had lifted, and the missing boat stood safely at anchor behind the breakwater, guided home in the dark by the steady sweep of the lamp. The fishermen left a basket of bread and smoked fish at the lighthouse door. The keeper ate her breakfast at the top of the tower, watching the tide go out, satisfied that the light had done its quiet work.
